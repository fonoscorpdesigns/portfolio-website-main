Country Recipe

UI/UX Design Case Study
Created with Figma
Duration: 9 weeks

About Country Recipe

A cooking app which gives users the opportunity to narrow their search by selecting the country they want to find a recipe from. Users will have the opportunity to get the missing ingredients needed to add to the ingredients they already have, the complete ingredients they need to make recipe(s) and also allow users to add their special recipe to the app

Design Approach

Brief and early ideation research

I browsed through a lot of cooking apps and found out that most cooking apps are the same, showing different recipes and ingredients needed, no room to add a favorite recipe, introducing notes and videos for learning and even having your daily weekly meal planner.

What I gathered

All these apps display the ingredients and recipe in general for viewers to see, but there is no room for users selecting the country they are from or the country they want to try their recipe. Also these app only allow users to add favorites and save recipes but they do not have section for users to add their own contribution of their special recipe for other users to try

Who are you designing for?
  • Users who can search recipes from other different country
  • Users who need to know how to prepare new recipes.
  • User can also contribute their special recipes to the app
What kinds of tasks and goals will your audience be accomplishing as they use your website or app?
  • Learning new recipes
  • Checking for missing ingredients
  • Checking for complete ingredients
  • Adding their special recipes
When will your audience be engaging with your products?
  • Daily
  • Each time they want to make a recipe
  • With friends to make dinner
Where will your audience be using what you’re designing?
  • At home
  • When they go for camping
  • With friends to make dinner
Why is your audience choosing to use what you’ve designed and what drives this behavior?
  • The option to select country of recipe
  • View missing ingredients to know the complete ingredients
  • Adding their own special recipe

Conducting a Usability Testing
I decided it is time I go for a usability test to put me in the right direction. From my experience, it is better to have your designs tested at the initial stage which is less stressful, avoid major errors and saves energy to start all over again. I reached out to friends and fellow designers to book an appointment with them for a usability testing

Study Guide

With the help and information gathered from the usability testing, I made changes to my design and take into consideration what the participants observed. This really helped because it gave me more insight about what am about to achieve and the benefit in making these changes So I decided to create a style guide for my final design using the inspiration i had from some cooking app (dribble, Behance) and from the participants I interviewed

Tweaks

  • I changed the slide navigations in the 3 welcoming pages to a standard navigation which users are user friendly with
  • I added a Register link to the Login Screen
  • Specified the income and expense categories in a different page for clarification
  • Made sure all final input shows on the record page
  • Created a button for EDIT, DELETE and INFO to Edit a record, Delete a record or navigate to the Analysis page through the Info button
  • Created a popup overlay to SET amount or DELETE record
  • A user can be able to start a new process by navigating to the HOME screen to start a new process inputting income and expenses
